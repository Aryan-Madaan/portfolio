Three lions and three wildebeest need to cross a river. There's one raft, and it holds at most **two animals**, and needs at least one aboard to paddle it.

The constraint: if lions ever **outnumber** wildebeest on either bank — even for a moment — the wildebeest get eaten. (A bank or the raft with zero wildebeest is always safe, no matter how many lions are there — there's nothing to outnumber.)

**Get all six animals across, without ever leaving a bank — or the raft — in a state where the wildebeest are outnumbered.**

<Spoiler>

It takes **11 one-way trips**, and the shape of the solution is the same shape that solves every version of this puzzle (missionaries and cannibals, jealous couples, whatever the cover story): **send pairs of the "dangerous" species over first, then bring the "safe" species over in bulk once it's no longer risky, using a few backtracking trips to keep both banks legal throughout.**

| # | Move | Left bank after | Right bank after |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | 2 lions cross → | 1L, 3W | 2L, 0W |
| 2 | 1 lion returns ← | 2L, 3W | 1L, 0W |
| 3 | 2 lions cross → | 0L, 3W | 3L, 0W |
| 4 | 1 lion returns ← | 1L, 3W | 2L, 0W |
| 5 | 2 wildebeest cross → | 1L, 1W | 2L, 2W |
| 6 | 1 lion + 1 wildebeest return ← | 2L, 2W | 1L, 1W |
| 7 | 2 wildebeest cross → | 2L, 0W | 1L, 3W |
| 8 | 1 lion returns ← | 3L, 0W | 0L, 3W |
| 9 | 2 lions cross → | 1L, 0W | 2L, 3W |
| 10 | 1 lion returns ← | 2L, 0W | 1L, 3W |
| 11 | 2 lions cross → | 0L, 0W | 3L, 3W |

Check every single row against the rule and nothing ever breaks it: every bank with wildebeest present always has wildebeest ≥ lions (rows 5, 6, 7, 9, 11 are the tight ones — each is exactly equal, 1-to-1 or 2-to-2 or 3-to-3, which is legal), and every bank with zero wildebeest is safe by definition regardless of how many lions pile up there (rows 1–4, 8, 10).

The part that feels wrong the first time you see it is step 6 — sending a lion *back* right after wildebeest just arrived, seemingly undoing progress. But it's necessary: after step 5, the far bank has 2 lions and 2 wildebeest, which is legal *only* because they arrived together in lockstep. To make further progress without ever letting lions get ahead on either bank, you have to temporarily retreat one of each species, re-open room on the near bank, and then bring the *rest* of the wildebeest across in one clean pair (step 7) while the lions are still held back in a safe ratio. The raft ping-pongs four times near the middle of the solution not because the animals are being inefficient, but because that's the only way to keep *both* banks simultaneously legal while still making net progress — the same reason the [bridge and torch puzzle](/puzzles/bridge-and-torch) needs a "wasteful-looking" return trip in the middle of its own optimal solution.

</Spoiler>
